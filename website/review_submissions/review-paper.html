Title: Ethical Dilemmas in Artificial Intelligence Deployment

In recent years, artificial intelligence (AI) has made significant strides, influencing nearly every industry from healthcare to law enforcement. While the benefits of automation, predictive analytics, and decision-making efficiency are widely acknowledged, the deployment of AI systems raises a number of ethical concerns that warrant careful attention.

This paper focuses on two major case studies: predictive policing algorithms in the United States and AI-assisted diagnostics in healthcare settings. In the former, we explore how historical bias embedded in training data can lead to disproportionate targeting of minority communities. For the latter, we examine how over-reliance on AI systems in hospitals can result in diagnostic oversight when human verification is sidelined.

Using the ethical frameworks of consequentialism and deontology, the paper evaluates the responsibilities of developers, governments, and institutions in the design and implementation of AI systems. For instance, is it ethically justifiable to use a highly accurate predictive policing system if it perpetuates systemic biases?
